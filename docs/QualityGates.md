# Quality Gates

## Objective

The purpose of these quality gates is to establish the minimum quality criteria required before testing activities can be considered complete and the product can be evaluated for release readiness.

The goal is not to eliminate all risk, but to ensure that critical business risks have been properly assessed and mitigated.

## Quality Gates

### Gate 1 – Priority 1 Coverage

All Priority 1 scenarios identified during the Risk Assessment phase must be executed.

### Gate 2 – Critical Defects

No critical severity defects may remain open at the end of the testing cycle.

### Gate 3 – Smoke Testing

Smoke test scenarios must execute successfully before release evaluation.

### Gate 4 – Test Evidence

Evidence of test execution must be available and properly documented.

Examples:
- Screenshots
- Test reports
- Execution logs
- Automation reports

### Gate 5 – Critical Area Coverage

All business-critical areas included in scope must receive testing coverage according to the defined Test Strategy.

## Release Recommendation

A release may be recommended when all quality gates have been satisfied and no unacceptable business risks remain open.

Any known risks must be documented and communicated to stakeholders before release approval.
