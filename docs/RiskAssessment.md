# Risk Assessment

## Critical Business Areas

| Area                     | Business Impact | Probability | Priority |
| ------------------------ | --------------- | ----------- | -------- |
| Login & Authentication   |       High      |    Medium   |    P1    |
| Employee Management      |       High      |     High    |    P1    |
| User Roles & Permissions |       High      | Medium-High |    P1    |
| Recruitment              |       High      |    Medium   |    P1    |
| Leave Management         |   Medium-High   |    Medium   |    P2    |
| Reporting                |      Medium     |    Medium   |    P2    |

## Conclusions
During the risk assessment process, six key business areas were identified and evaluated. Four of them were classified as the highest priority (P1): Login & Authentication, Employee Management, User Roles & Permissions, and Recruitment.

These modules were prioritized due to their significant impact on business operations. They directly affect system access, employee data integrity, security controls, and core business processes. A failure in any of these areas could significantly disrupt business continuity and operational efficiency.

Leave Management and Reporting were classified as medium priority (P2). While both areas are important for day-to-day operations and business visibility, their failures are less likely to completely block business activities and may have temporary manual workarounds.

This risk assessment follows a risk-based testing approach, prioritizing validation efforts in areas that present the highest impact to business continuity, security, data integrity, and operational efficiency. The results of this assessment will be used to guide future test design, automation priorities, and overall quality assurance activities throughout the project.
